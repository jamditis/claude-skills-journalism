Crisis communications

Frameworks for accurate, rapid communication during breaking news and high-pressure situations.

When to use

  • Breaking news requires immediate coverage
  • Organization faces public crisis or controversy
  • Misinformation is spreading rapidly and needs countering
  • Emergency situation requires coordinated communication
  • Preparing crisis response plans before incidents occur

What's included

Breaking news protocol

First 30 minutes checklist, verification phase workflow, newsroom escalation matrix with crisis levels 1-4.

Rapid verification

5-minute verification checklist, verification triage system with priority queues, claim tracking workflow.

Communication templates

Holding statements, corrections, clarifications, retractions, and status update templates for stakeholders.

Social media response

Crisis monitoring dashboard, response decision tree, guidelines for when to engage vs. when to ignore.

Misinformation response

Counter-messaging framework: lead with truth, avoid repeating myths, provide authoritative sources.

Post-crisis review

After-action checklist, timeline reconstruction, process improvement documentation.

Escalation levels

Level 1

Routine

Single reporter can handle. Standard verification and publication workflow.

Level 2

Elevated

Editor involvement needed. Triggers: multiple fatalities, major public figure, legal concerns, significant local impact.

Level 3

Major

Multiple reporters, editor oversight. Triggers: national news potential, active public danger, major institution affected, coordinated misinformation.

Level 4

Critical

All hands, executive involvement. Triggers: mass casualty event, government/democracy implications, organization directly involved, imminent physical threat.

5-minute verification checklist

Source check (1 min)

  • Who is claiming this?
  • Are they in position to know?
  • Have they been reliable before?

Corroboration (2 min)

  • Does anyone else confirm?
  • Check wire services
  • Check official sources

Red flags (1 min)

  • Too perfect/dramatic?
  • Matches known false narratives?
  • Single source only?

Decision (1 min)

  • PUBLISH: Multiple credible sources, no red flags
  • HOLD: Needs more verification
  • MONITOR: Developing, don't publish yet
